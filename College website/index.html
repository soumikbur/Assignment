Welcome to India

India's heritage is a rich tapestry of culture and traditions that have been passed down through generations. India is home to many ethnic groups, each with their own unique customs and traditions. Some traditions remain the same across the country, while others are specific to certain communities. India also has many World Heritage Sites, including cultural sites, natural sites, and cities: Cultural sites These include the Taj Mahal, which is considered a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic architecture, and other sites like the Agra Fort, Ajanta Caves, and Ellora Caves. Natural sites These include Kaziranga National Park, which is home to the world's largest population of Indian rhinoceroses, and other sites like the Great Himalayan National Park Conservation Area and the Western Ghats. Cities Ahmedabad was the first Indian city to be recognized as a World Heritage City by UNESCO.
